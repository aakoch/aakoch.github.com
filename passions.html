Passions

Clean Code

The book Clean Code by "Uncle" Robert Martin changed my life.

I also really enjoy reviewing code.

Teaching

I've starting to realize that I love sharing things. At my company I teach classes, volunteer to be on panels, post my findings/research on the wiki, and send emails with tips and tricks. The only problem is, I'm not a very good speaker. I'm not good with things coming up right off the top of my head. I'm better at a "longer" medium where I can collect my thoughts before presenting them.

User Interface Design

I used to work on the User Experience team which included Human Factors, the team with degrees in Human-Computer Interaction. Before working with that team I thought, as I expect most developers without that kind of exposure, that my interfaces were fine. But I believe I learned a lot and have gained "an eye" for bad UI design.

Security

I'm am facinated and often amazed with the abilities of hackers and sometimes contemplate what vulnerabilities are in my apps.

New Technologies

From new hardware devices to electronics to software projects, I get excited reading about advances in technology. I like to try them out BUT, and that's a big but, I don't always think the cutting edge is right for the enterprise.
